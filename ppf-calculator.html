How PPF returns are calculated

Public Provident Fund (PPF) has a fixed lock-in period of 15 years. Interest is compounded annually and applied to the balance every year. The final maturity value depends on yearly contributions and the prevailing interest rate.

Formula used

Each year’s investment grows with compound interest:

A = P × ( ( (1 + r)n − 1 ) / r ) × (1 + r)

where P is yearly investment, r is annual interest rate, and n is 15 years.

Example

If you invest ₹1,50,000 every year for 15 years at an interest rate of 7.1%, this calculator estimates the total invested amount and the maturity value at the end of the tenure.
