SIP Calculator

This SIP calculator estimates the future value of your monthly investments by applying monthly compounding to each installment. It is designed for practical planning — retirement targets, education funds, or any long-term goal where regular contributions matter. Enter your monthly contribution, expected annual return, and duration to see an estimated maturity amount and the total invested sum.

When to use this calculator

Use this calculator when you want to convert a monthly savings target into a long-term corpus, or when you want to compare different monthly amounts and tenures quickly. It’s particularly helpful if you are choosing between starting a SIP versus a lump-sum investment, or when deciding how much to increase monthly savings to reach a target by a certain year.

How it works (plain explanation)

The calculator converts the entered annual return into a monthly rate (annual ÷ 12 ÷ 100) and compounds each monthly contribution at that monthly rate for the remaining months until maturity. Earlier contributions compound for longer, so duration has a large effect on the final amount. The computation uses the standard SIP maturity approach applied iteratively month-by-month for accuracy.

Benefits of SIP investing

SIPs enforce disciplined investing — you invest regularly regardless of market conditions, which helps average your purchase price over time (rupee-cost averaging). SIPs also harness compound interest: small monthly amounts, invested consistently over many years, can grow substantially. They are flexible (you can change amounts and stop/start), low-effort, and suitable for long-term goals.

Factors that affect SIP returns

Key factors include the monthly amount, investment duration, and the actual returns achieved by the underlying funds. Market volatility means returns vary year-to-year; higher average returns and longer durations boost the final corpus. Fund selection, expense ratios, and taxes also impact net returns. Use conservative estimates when planning to avoid unrealistic expectations.

Frequently asked questions

Is SIP better than lump sum? It depends — SIPs help if you want to average into the market and avoid timing risk; lump sum can outperform if markets rise steadily and you have capital available.

Can I stop SIP anytime? Yes — most platforms allow you to pause or stop SIPs without penalties; check fund exit loads and tax implications.

Are returns guaranteed? No — mutual funds are market-linked. This calculator provides estimates based on the return you enter; actual outcomes vary.
