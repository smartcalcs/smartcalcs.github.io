What is CAGR?

CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) represents the average annual growth rate of an investment assuming it grows at a steady rate every year.

How CAGR is calculated

CAGR = (Final Value / Initial Value)1 / Years − 1

Example

If an investment grows from ₹1,00,000 to ₹2,50,000 in 5 years, the CAGR shows the average annual growth rate over that period.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is CAGR the same as actual returns?

No. CAGR assumes steady growth every year. Actual investments fluctuate, but CAGR helps compare long-term performance.

Can CAGR be negative?

Yes. If the final value is lower than the initial value, the CAGR will be negative, indicating an average annual loss.

Is CAGR useful for mutual funds?

Yes. CAGR is commonly used to measure mutual fund, stock, and long-term investment performance.
