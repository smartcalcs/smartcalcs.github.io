How inflation affects money

Inflation reduces the purchasing power of money over time. As prices rise, the same amount of money buys fewer goods and services.

Inflation Formula

FV = P × (1 + r)t

Where:
P = Current amount
r = Annual inflation rate (decimal)
t = Time in years

Example

If inflation is 6% per year, ₹1,00,000 today will have the purchasing power of approximately ₹55,800 after 10 years.
